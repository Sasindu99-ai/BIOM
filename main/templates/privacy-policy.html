1. Introduction

Welcome to BIOM (the "Platform"). BIOM is a biomarker and study management platform designed to facilitate research and data management in the field of biomarkers and clinical studies. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our platform.

By accessing or using BIOM, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with the practices described in this policy, please do not use our platform.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you:

  • Register for an account on BIOM
  • Participate in studies or research activities
  • Contact us with inquiries or support requests
  • Subscribe to our newsletters or updates

This information may include:

  • Name and contact information (email address, phone number)
  • Professional affiliation and credentials
  • Account credentials and authentication information
  • Biographical and demographic information as required for research participation

2.2 Study and Research Data

As a research platform, BIOM processes various types of study-related data including:

  • Biomarker information and characterization data
  • Patient and participant information (collected in compliance with applicable regulations)
  • Study variables, results, and analysis data
  • Research findings and associated metadata

2.3 Automatically Collected Information

When you access BIOM, we automatically collect certain information including:

  • IP address and device identifiers
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Usage patterns, pages viewed, and time spent on the platform
  • Referring website addresses
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

3.1 Service Provision

  • To create, maintain, and manage your account
  • To provide access to BIOM features and functionality
  • To process and manage studies, biomarkers, and research data
  • To facilitate collaboration between researchers and participants

3.2 Research and Analysis

  • To conduct research in accordance with applicable research protocols
  • To analyze and aggregate data for scientific and statistical purposes
  • To generate reports and insights for authorized users
  • To improve our platform and develop new features

3.3 Communication

  • To respond to your inquiries and provide customer support
  • To send administrative information and updates about BIOM
  • To deliver research-related communications and notifications
  • To provide educational content and newsletters (with your consent)

3.4 Compliance and Legal

  • To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and research ethics requirements
  • To enforce our Terms and Conditions and protect our rights
  • To prevent fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access
  • To respond to legal requests and protect public health

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

4.1 Research Collaborators

With your consent or as authorized by your participation agreement, we may share study data with authorized researchers, study collaborators, and institutions involved in approved research projects.

4.2 Service Providers

We may engage third-party service providers to assist with platform operations, including cloud storage, data analytics, and technical support. These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data.

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by law, court order, or governmental authority, including:

  • Compliance with health data regulations (e.g., HIPAA, GDPR)
  • Response to subpoenas or legal process
  • Protection of rights, safety, or property
  • Public health and safety concerns

4.4 Aggregated and De-identified Data

We may share aggregated, de-identified, or anonymized data for research purposes, publication, or platform improvement without identifying individual participants.

5. Data Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms
  • Regular security assessments and monitoring
  • Employee training on data protection
  • Secure data backup and recovery procedures

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information:

6.1 Access and Portability

  • Request access to your personal data held by BIOM
  • Receive a copy of your data in a portable format
  • Review and verify the accuracy of your information

6.2 Correction and Deletion

  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Request deletion of your personal information (subject to legal and research requirements)
  • Withdraw consent for certain processing activities

6.3 Objection and Restriction

  • Object to specific uses of your personal data
  • Request restriction of processing in certain circumstances

6.4 Account Controls

  • Update your account information and preferences
  • Manage communication preferences and opt-out of marketing emails
  • Close or deactivate your account (subject to research obligations)

Note: Some rights may be limited by research agreements, legal requirements, or the legitimate interests of scientific research. For study participants, withdrawal rights will be explained in the informed consent documentation.

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

BIOM uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience, analyze platform usage, and provide personalized features. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings.

We use cookies for:

  • Authentication and session management
  • Platform functionality and personalization
  • Analytics and performance monitoring

8. Children's Privacy

BIOM is not intended for use by children under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected data from a child without appropriate consent, we will take steps to delete that information.

For studies involving minors, additional parental or guardian consent procedures will be implemented in accordance with applicable laws and research ethics guidelines.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be processed and stored in countries outside your jurisdiction. When transferring data internationally, we implement appropriate safeguards to ensure your data receives adequate protection in accordance with applicable privacy laws.

10. Research Ethics and Compliance

BIOM is committed to conducting research in accordance with ethical standards and applicable regulations:

  • Compliance with institutional review board (IRB) requirements
  • Adherence to health data protection regulations (HIPAA, GDPR, etc.)
  • Implementation of informed consent procedures
  • Protection of participant confidentiality and rights
  • Transparent research practices and data handling

11. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. For research data, retention periods are determined by:

  • Research protocols and study requirements
  • Legal and regulatory obligations
  • Scientific and archival purposes
  • Statute of limitations considerations

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. We will notify you of material changes by:

  • Posting a prominent notice on BIOM
  • Sending an email to registered users
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy

Your continued use of BIOM after changes become effective constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

14. Additional Rights for Certain Jurisdictions

14.1 European Union / GDPR

If you are located in the European Union, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

14.2 California / CCPA

California residents have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what personal information is collected and the right to opt-out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell personal information).

14.3 Health Information / HIPAA

For health-related information subject to HIPAA, additional protections and procedures apply as specified in applicable business associate agreements and research authorizations.
